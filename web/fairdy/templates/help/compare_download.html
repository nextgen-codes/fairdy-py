View, Compare, and Download Simulations

In addition to running and storing individual storage system life cycle simulations, you are also able to compare the results of several simulations simultaneously and download the raw simulation data.

Both the compare and download features are controlled by the checkboxes and buttons on the main simulation index page. Simply check the boxes you wish to compare or download, and click the appropriate button. Your selections will be stored as you navigate between different page numbers.

View

After a simulation is finished processing you will be redirected to a page where you can view the results. This page includes a plot of the Block Availability Factor throughout the evolution of your system, as well as details of the system itself.

The evolution of a storage system shown in terms of BAF during each cycle

Compare

To view and compare the results of several simulations simultaneously, check the box for the relevant simulations followed by the "Compare" Button.

Selecting two Reed-Solomon systems for comparison
Comparing these two simulations with otherwise identical specifications we see that the lazy heal threshold of 16 has a serious negative effect on the evolution of the system over time.

Download

You can download the results of any simulation in a comma separated values (CSV) file to analyze or plot the data in software of your choice. The data includes the parameters used to define the storage system and simulation, along with 3 pieces of data for each of the simulation's cycles: block availability factor, number of blocks faulted this cycle, and number of blocks healed this cycle.
